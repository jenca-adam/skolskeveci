AYOREO PEOPLE

1.0 Introduction

Ayoreo people are nomadic hunter-gatherer indigenous tribe living in Gran Chaco on borders of Paraguay and Bolivia.
Their population is around 5,600 . There are small groups of them (around 100 in total) that remain uncontacted.

1.1 Voluntarily isolated groups

Uncontacted groups are voluntarily isolated. They don't really want to see modern civilisation,
but they are threatened by massive deforestation and cattle farming. They struggle to avoid contact, they even need to communicate with whistles because they don't want to be detected. When deforestation came too far and they had only small patches of forest between fields to hide,
a small group was forced to make contact. Finally, they had to give up their culture. As result of this contact, several health issues began.
