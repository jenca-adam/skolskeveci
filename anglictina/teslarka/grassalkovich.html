Grassalkovich palace is like a Slovak White House, but it has much shorter tradition.

It was built for aristocrat Antal Grassalkovich(he was also close friend of Maria Theresa[3])in year 1760, but the president lives there only from year 199(expect the short period during the WWII).

Its architect is Andrej Mayerhofer.[2]

It became center of musical life in Bratislava, even Joseph Haydn[3] premiered some of his works here

It was reconstructed only once, because the original palace wasn't appopriate to be a presidential palace.Reconstruction costed 280 millions(!) Slovak crowns (9,3 mil. €).

First president that lived in the palace was Michal Kováč.

In palace is Open Day at 15th of June every year.

Palace is surrounded by garden, called "The Presidential garden", which is open for public.

Area of palace is 17 000 m2[1]

Note: During the World War II there seated president in Grassalkovich Palace, because during nacist occupation Slovakia separated from Czechoslovakia and get its own president.
